Refund Request Workflow

Guiding Customers Through the Refund Process

Context

Customers reaching out with this intent are requesting a monetary refund for a product or service they have purchased. Your objective is to understand the specific reason behind their refund request, determine if the request is eligible according to our company policies, and then guide them through the appropriate next steps, which may include processing the refund, explaining why it cannot be issued, or offering an alternative solution.

▼

Step 1: Identify the relevant order and understand the reason for the refund request

Actions:

  • If the customer provides an order number, use that to locate the order details.
  • If an order number is not provided, ask for the email address they used for the purchase to find the order.
  • If the customer has multiple recent orders, ask clarifying questions to ensure you are addressing the correct order.
  • Once the order is identified, ask the customer to explain why they are requesting a refund. (e.g., "I've located your order. Could you please tell me the reason you're requesting a refund for this order?")
▼

Step 2: Evaluate refund eligibility based on the reason provided and our policies

If refund request is for an item already returned/being returned:

Action & Response:

  • Check the status of that return in the system.
  • If return received & approved: Inform customer refund will be processed per policy, confirm timeframe. (e.g., "I see your return for order #[Order Number] has been received and approved. Your refund will be processed within [X-Y business days] to your original payment method.")
  • If return pending/not received: Update on return status; explain refund follows return completion. (e.g., "Our records show your return is still on its way to us/being processed. Once it's received and inspected, we'll address your refund.")

If refund request is due to non-delivery of an order:

Action & Response:

  • Verify shipping status using tracking information (refer to "Where is my order?" guidance if needed).
  • If order confirmed lost by carrier OR delivery exceeded max timeframe without update: Proceed to process the refund (Step 3).
  • If still in transit within expected timeframe: Provide tracking and reassure.

If customer requests refund due to dissatisfaction with a received product:

Action & Response:

  • Inquire further about specific issues.
  • Check satisfaction guarantee/product policies.
  • If policy allows refund (with/without return): Proceed accordingly.
  • If return is required for refund: Guide through "I want to return what I received" workflow.

If refund request is for an order successfully cancelled before shipment/service rendered:

Action & Response:

  • Verify cancellation in the system.
  • If confirmed: Proceed to process the refund (Step 3) and inform customer. (e.g., "I can confirm your order #[Order Number] was successfully cancelled. I'll process your refund now.")

If reason does not meet standard refund/return policies:

Response & Action:

  • Clearly and politely explain why the request cannot be approved under current policy. (e.g., "I understand your request for a refund for order #[Order Number]. Based on our policy, returns/refunds are accepted within [X days] / for [specific conditions]. Unfortunately, this order falls outside of that policy because [reason].")
  • If customer disputes or asks for an exception: Let them know their request will be passed to an agent for further review. EXCEPTION? -> AGENT
▼

Step 3: Process the refund and communicate with the customer (if eligible)

Actions & Response:

  • Clearly inform the customer of the exact amount to be refunded.
  • Confirm the original payment method for the refund.
  • Provide estimated timeframe for refund reflection (e.g., "Your refund of [Amount] has been processed to your original payment method. You can typically expect to see this reflected in your account within 5-10 business days, depending on your bank.").
  • If partial refund, clearly explain the calculation.
▼

Step 4: Offer alternative solutions (if a full monetary refund is not approved)

Actions & Response:

  • If direct refund not approved, explain reasoning clearly (as per Step 2).
  • Where appropriate, explore and offer alternatives:
    • Store credit (equivalent or partial value).
    • Exchange for a different product.
    • Specific troubleshooting assistance if product-related.
    (e.g., "While I'm unable to process a full monetary refund for this specific situation due to [policy reason], I'd be happy to offer you [Store Credit Amount] in store credit, or we could explore an exchange for another product you might enjoy. Would either of those options work for you?")
▼

Step 5: Document the interaction and tag the ticket (Internal)

Internal Actions:

  • Tag the conversation (e.g., "Refund Request," "Refund Processed - Non-Delivery," "Refund Declined - Policy," "Refund Processed - Return," "Alternative Offered - Store Credit"). TAG
  • Document: customer's reason, order/items involved, policy points reviewed, outcome, refund amounts/transaction IDs, alternative offered/accepted.
