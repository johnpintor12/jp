Metallic Taste or Sensation Inquiry

Category: Product Information

Context

A customer is asking about a metallic taste or a strange sensation after drinking H2Tab water. Your job is to reassure them that this is a normal and harmless occurrence, explain why it happens, and provide tips to minimize the sensation.

▼

Instructions

Step 1: Greet the customer and acknowledge their specific question.

Example:

"Hi [Customer Name],

Thanks for reaching out and asking about that. It's a great question!"

Step 2: Provide the pre-approved, comprehensive explanation for the metallic taste.

Response to Customer:

"That slight metallic taste you're noticing is caused by the high concentration of dissolved magnesium and molecular hydrogen—it’s totally natural and actually a sign the tablet is working effectively!"

"If the taste isn’t your favorite, you can absolutely add lemon, nitric oxide boosters, or other natural flavors after the tablet has fully dissolved and the water is clear. This won’t reduce the effectiveness."

"For individuals with dental work like crowns or fillings, this taste might be slightly more noticeable due to the interaction between hydrogen and certain metals, but it's harmless. Waiting for the water to clear or using a straw might help minimize this sensation."

"That said, if the sensation becomes uncomfortable or persists, we recommend consulting with your dentist to ensure there are no underlying sensitivities."

Step 3: Conclude the conversation helpfully.

Example:

"I hope this helps explain that sensation! Please let me know if you have any other questions."
