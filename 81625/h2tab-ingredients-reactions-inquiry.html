H2Tab Ingredients & Reactions Inquiry

Category: Product Information

Context

A customer is asking about the ingredients in H2Tab, potential side effects, or allergic reactions. Your job is to provide clear, accurate, and reassuring information based on pre-approved statements, addressing their specific concerns about ingredients like dextrose or magnesium, and explaining common, harmless sensations like a metallic taste.

▼

Step 1: Identify the Specific Type of Inquiry

Decision Logic:

Analyze the customer's message for keywords to determine the primary nature of their question.

  • If keywords relate to **"dextrose," "sugar," "sweetener"**: Focus on the Dextrose explanation.
  • If keywords relate to **"magnesium," "malic acid," "ingredients list"**: Focus on the full ingredient list.
  • If keywords relate to **"metallic taste," "weird taste," "sensation"**: Focus on the Metallic Taste explanation.
  • If keywords relate to **"safe," "side effects," "allergic reaction"**: Focus on the general safety statement.
  • If the query is general, provide the full ingredient list first.
▼

Step 2: Provide Pre-Approved Information Based on Inquiry Type

If the customer asks about Dextrose:

"Thanks for asking! We include a very small, trace amount of dextrose in our tablets. It serves a critical role as a binder to hold the tablet together and helps to kickstart the hydrogen-producing reaction when it hits the water. The amount is minimal and is consumed during the reaction itself, so it doesn't contribute to the sugar content of the final hydrogen water you drink. However, we completely understand that some of our customers prefer to avoid dextrose entirely, and we've taken that feedback seriously. Our team is actively working on developing a dextrose-free version, and we hope to have it available in the future!"

If the customer asks for the ingredient list:

"Of course! The ingredients in H2Tab are straightforward and selected for their purity and effectiveness. Each tablet contains a proprietary blend of: Magnesium, Malic Acid, Dextrose, Tartaric Acid, Adipic Acid, and Sodium Stearyl Fumarate. These ingredients work together to create the therapeutic molecular hydrogen when the tablet dissolves in water."

If the customer asks about a metallic taste:

"That's a great question, and something a few of our customers notice! That slight metallic taste is caused by the high concentration of dissolved magnesium and molecular hydrogen—it’s totally natural and actually a sign the tablet is working effectively! If the taste isn’t your favorite, you can absolutely add lemon, nitric oxide boosters, or other natural flavors after the tablet has fully dissolved and the water is clear. This won’t reduce the effectiveness. For individuals with dental work like crowns or fillings, this taste might be slightly more noticeable due to the interaction between hydrogen and certain metals, but it's harmless. Waiting for the water to clear or using a straw might help minimize this sensation."

If the customer asks about general safety:

"Yes, H2Tab is made from safe, high-quality ingredients and is designed to support your health without any harmful side effects. Molecular hydrogen itself has no known adverse effects and is considered extremely safe for daily use."

▼

Step 3: Offer Further Assistance and Conclude

End the interaction with:

"Please let me know if you have any other questions or if there's anything else we can assist you with!"
