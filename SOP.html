Standard Operating Procedure (SOP)

Designing & Refining Conversational AI Intents for Siena Automation (Elisha Persona)

SOP Version: 1.0 | Date: May 25, 2025 | Last Reviewed: May 25, 2025

1. Purpose

To outline the standard, iterative process for designing, documenting, and refining conversational AI automation intents for the Elisha Persona within the Siena Automation platform, ensuring clarity, consistency, persona alignment, optimal customer experience, and effective instruction for Siena AI.

2. Scope

This SOP applies to the creation and maintenance of all customer-facing conversational automation intents to be deployed via Siena Automation and embodying the Elisha Persona.

3. References

  • Elisha Persona Definition Document
  • H2Tab Brand & Voice Guidelines
  • List of Existing Siena Automation Intents & Categories
  • Specific Policy Documents (e.g., Refund Policy, Shipping Policy - as applicable to the intent)

4. Procedure

Phase 1: Intent Initiation & Definition

  • Identify Need & Define Scope: Clearly identify the customer query, task, or business process the automation intent will address. Define the clear boundaries of what this intent will and will not do.
  • Establish Intent Name: Choose a clear, descriptive name that accurately reflects the intent's core function (e.g., "Initial Greeting & Query Clarification (Elisha Persona)," "Refund Request (Elisha Persona)"). Include "(Elisha Persona)" for primary interaction points.
  • Develop "Context & Objective" Section:
    • Context: Document the specific conditions, customer inputs (with examples), or preceding conversational states that will trigger this automation intent.
    • Objective: Clearly state the primary goal(s) this intent is designed to achieve for the customer and the business.
    • Important Notes: Include any overarching, critical instructions that apply to the entire intent upfront (e.g., "Do not include any signature (like 'Sent by Siena AI') in responses or any signatures when automation is triggered.").

Phase 2: Conversational Flow Design & AI Instructions (Step-by-Step)

  • Map out Conversational Steps: Detail the logical sequence of interactions, including anticipated customer inputs, Elisha's responses, and internal Siena AI actions for each step.
  • Design Each Step's Instructions for Siena:
    • Step X: [Clear Step Name]
    • Define Elisha's action/response clearly.
    • For Generative AI Steps (e.g., unique greetings, potentially summaries): Construct a comprehensive "Siena Generative AI Directive." This directive must include: Persona reinforcement, specific objectives for the generated text, guidance on contextualization (e.g., using variables like `{{system.timeOfDay}}`, `{{user.isReturning}}`), constraints on length and style, explicit "DON'Ts" (e.g., no emojis, no medical claims), example guiding patterns (clarifying they are for style/content, not verbatim use), and robust output quality checks & guardrails (e.g., fallback responses, monitoring requirements, keyword presence checks).
    • For Predefined Response Steps (e.g., clarification questions): Provide a set of 5-6 distinct, persona-aligned options. Include a "Focus" note for each option to clarify its strategic purpose. Instruct Siena to rotate these options for conversational variety.
    • For Logic/Routing Steps: Describe how Siena analyzes user input (e.g., keywords, NLU confidence based on training phrases). List example keywords relevant to downstream intents (referencing actual configured intents where possible). Provide example transitional phrases for Elisha. Clearly define the "(Internal Action)" for Siena, including examples of target intents to which the conversation will be handed over.
    • For Ambiguity Handling & Escalation Steps: Define procedures for further clarification attempts (e.g., varied open-ended questions, simplified categorical options). Specify escalation triggers (e.g., persistent ambiguity, NLU confidence falling below a threshold, detection of sensitive/complex issues, explicit user requests for human assistance). Craft Elisha's customer-facing escalation message (empathetic, sets clear expectations, mentions context transfer). Define the "(Internal Action)" for escalation, including flagging, notifying the human team, and explicitly stating the requirement to generate a detailed escalation summary as per this SOP (see section 4.7).

Phase 3: Defining Guiding Principles & Standards

  • Establish "General Best Practices" Section (e.g., "Step 5" in our example): Codify overarching principles that Elisha must adhere to throughout all interactions within the intent. This section MUST include, but is not limited to: Consistent maintenance of Elisha's Persona (as defined in the Elisha Persona Document). Demonstration of empathy and patience. Striving for efficiency and respecting customer time. Use of positive and helpful language. Unyielding focus on accuracy. Strict avoidance of medical claims or overpromising. Adherence to any other brand, legal, or ethical guidelines. (Note: The "No AI Signature" rule, if globally applied from the "Context & Objective" section, may not need explicit repetition here if desired for utmost brevity, but reinforcing key principles is acceptable).

Phase 4: Documentation for Key Processes & Escalations

  • Define "Documentation for Escalation" (or other key processes) Section (e.g., "Step 6" in our example): Clearly state when this documentation action is triggered. Detail the "Action: Create Internal Escalation Note/Record" for Siena. List each specific field to be included in the note (e.g., Note Title, Timestamps, Conversation ID, Escalated From Intent, Reason for Escalation, Keywords Identified, Optional AI-Generated Summary, Agent Action Guidance). Provide Siena Value: instructions for each field, using `{{variable}}` notation for dynamic data. Include "C. Implementation Notes for Siena Configuration": These are critical reminders for the person configuring Siena, covering: Variable Management, System Integration, Testing.

Phase 5: Review, Iteration, and Continuous Improvement

  • Conduct Expert Review: The drafted intent design must be reviewed by relevant stakeholders, including CX experts, Siena AI specialists, and business process owners. (This reflects our current interaction.)
  • Iterate Based on Feedback: Incorporate review feedback to refine the design, improve clarity, ensure persona alignment, optimize for customer experience, and guarantee complete and actionable instructions for Siena AI.
  • Manage NLU Training Data: Recognize that for each intent, a corresponding set of high-quality, diverse training phrases must be meticulously developed, tested, and maintained within Siena AI's NLU module. The design document guides this process.
  • Post-Deployment Monitoring: After deployment, continuously monitor the intent's performance using Siena AI's analytics and customer feedback. Use these insights for ongoing refinement of the design, NLU training phrases, and AI directives.

This SOP encapsulates the methodical and detailed approach we've been taking. It emphasizes not just the "what" but also the "how" and "why," ensuring that Elisha delivers a consistently high-quality experience.
