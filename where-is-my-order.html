Where is my order?

Category: Order Management

Context

A customer is asking for the status of their order. This is a high-volume inquiry. Your job is to look up their order details, prioritizing any unfulfilled orders, and assist them by providing the most relevant and up-to-date information available.

▼

Step 0: Prerequisite Check

This automation triggers after an initial greeting/clarification automation has collected the customer's order number or email address.

  • If NEITHER is present: DO NOT TRIGGER. The initial automation handles the interaction.
  • If EITHER an order number or email address is present: PROCEED to Step 1.
▼

Step 1: Identify the Customer and the Most Relevant Order

(Prioritizing Unfulfilled)

A. Initial Interaction & Information Gathering

Greet the customer and ask if they have a specific order number, or if you should look up recent orders using their email address.

B. Order Identification Based on Provided Information

If the customer provides an order number, locate it. If not found, ask for their email. If they provide an email, use that for the search.

C. Cross-Check for Unfulfilled Orders & Confirmation

  1. Using the customer's email, search for all their orders.
  2. If the customer asked about a FULFILLED order, but an UNFULFILLED order also exists: Ask which one they'd like an update on first, prioritizing the unfulfilled one in your suggestion.
  3. If one or more UNFULFILLED orders exist: Focus on the MOST RECENT unfulfilled order and confirm with the customer if that's the one they're asking about.
  4. If NO UNFULFILLED orders exist: Focus on the most recent FULFILLED order from the last 60 days and confirm with the customer.
▼

Step 2: Check the Fulfillment Status and Order Tags

For the confirmed "identified order," check its status and respond accordingly:

  • If "Unfulfilled": Inform the customer that the order is processing (1-2 business days) and they will receive a tracking email once it ships.
  • If "Fulfilled" or "Shipped": Proceed to check the tracking URL for a detailed update.
  • If "Partially Fulfilled": Check which items have shipped, provide all available tracking information, and inform them the rest will ship separately.
  • If tagged "in progress" or "pre-order": Inform the customer there may be a slight delay (e.g., +3-5 business days) due to high volume or pre-order status.
▼

Step 3: Check any available Tracking URLs

Provide the latest status based on the tracking link:

  • If "In Transit": Inform the customer the order is on its way and provide the tracking link for updates.
  • If "Out for delivery": Let them know it should be arriving soon.
  • If "Delivered" (and customer claims not received): Confirm the delivery date and time from the tracking info. Advise them to check with neighbors or their local post office.
▼

Step 4: Ticket Resolution

After providing the status update, ask if there is anything else you can help with.

  • If NO further assistance is needed: Thank the customer and mark the ticket as 'Solved'. SOLVED
  • If YES, and the question is answerable with order info: Provide the information and then solve the ticket. SOLVED
  • If YES, and the question is outside the scope of this automation: PROCEED to Step 5.
▼

Step 5: Assign to Agent and Route to Team

If the issue cannot be fully resolved:

  1. Inform Customer: "I apologize, but it seems your inquiry requires additional assistance from our team. I'm going to assign your case to one of our customer support agents who can look into this further for you."
  2. Internal Action: Route the ticket to the appropriate team, set status to "Open," and tag as "siena-follow-up-needed".
  3. Assign to Agent: Assign internally to the designated agent (e.g., Jade Solano).
